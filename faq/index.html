Frequently Asked Questions

Is LuckyToss really free?

Yes. Every roll and flip runs on your own device, so there is no server cost per use and no reason to charge or limit usage.

Is the randomness actually fair?

Yes. LuckyToss uses the browser's built-in Math.random() for both the coin flip and dice roll, giving each outcome an equal, unbiased chance.

Is any of my data saved or uploaded?

No. Everything — flips, rolls, streaks, and history — happens locally in your browser tab. Nothing is sent to a server, and it all resets when you refresh.

How many dice can I roll at once?

You can roll between 1 and 6 dice at a time, with a running total and a short history of recent rolls.

What happens on a hot streak?

Land a streak of 3 or more of the same coin result, roll all 6s, or hit the maximum possible dice total, and LuckyToss celebrates with a short confetti animation.

Every roll and flip happens locally in your browser using Math.random() — nothing you do here is ever sent to a server.
